# RKM Foundation — Emotional Direction
### victorfuruya.com studied as an emotional storytelling system, translated to animal rescue

You're not chasing the design — you're chasing the feeling. Furuya's site earns four feelings; here's what creates each, and how RKM earns the same with its **own** brand, colours, copy, and content.

| Feeling | Furuya creates it by… | RKM translation |
|---|---|---|
| **Quality** | Weighted, deliberate scroll · restraint · flawless spacing · premium type | Light momentum scroll site-wide · one idea per screen · the editorial spacing already approved |
| **Trust** | Confidence — never shouts or sells; assumes you're interested; total consistency | Lead with the animal, not the ask · understate the 12A/80G proof · let craft signal credibility |
| **Professionalism** | Editorial structure · numeric wayfinding · meta-labels · alignment discipline | Chapter numbers on rescue stories · "(Rescued) Thane · 2025" meta-labels · strict alignment |
| **Emotional engagement** | Story over data · one subject at a time · photography carries meaning · scroll = pacing | The animal is the subject · one beat per scroll · real rescue photos · numbers as emotion, not stats |

**The core swap:** Furuya's subject is the designer. **RKM's subject is the rescued animal.** Same system — empathy instead of abstraction.

---

## 1. Homepage concept — from "webpage" to "trailer"
Keep every section, colour, and word. Change the *feel*: weighted scroll, one idea per viewport, the animal's face dominant (the approved hero direction, pushed further), copper as a single warm accent, lower information density. The homepage's job becomes to **pull you into the impact stories** — not to explain everything at once.

## 2. Impact-story concept — the biggest missed opportunity
A dedicated, scrollable **"Rescues"** experience: each animal is an editorial chapter — word → photo → line → photo → number → outcome → donate. Emotion first; the stats live *inside* the story, never in a grid. This becomes RKM's emotional engine, its literal "you see where it goes" proof, and its most shareable content.

## 3. Donation-page concept — the last beat of a story, not a form
Translate confidence + comprehension: open with a single rescued-animal image and "Your gift, today" — not a dense form. Keep the tiers + the amount→impact slider, but give them room and show the security cue at the decision point. Fewer things on screen; one clear path. It should feel like the natural **conclusion of an impact story**, not a checkout.

## 4. Rescue-story concept — the repeatable template *(built as a live mockup)*
Your storyboard, realised: **Rescued. → found injured beside a highway → 4 surgeries → 178 days → today she runs again → Donate ₹500, help the next rescue.** One beat per screen, real photos, almost no text. **Every rescued animal gets one** — a template, not a one-off. See `RKMF-RescueStory-Concept.html` (scroll it).

## 5. Before / after visual direction
- **Before:** conventional charity page — several things per screen, mid-size photos, information density, a "please donate" tone.
- **After:** editorial — one idea per screen, full-bleed photography, weighted scroll, confident restraint, copper as a single accent, story-led.
- **Same brand, colours, content — different feeling.** Nothing is redrawn; the composition and pacing carry the premium.

## 6. Mobile experience — where this actually wins
Most donors are on mobile, and **one-beat-per-screen is native to mobile** (vertical, full-bleed, story-like — editorial, not a "stories" gimmick). Light momentum scroll (never scroll-lock), big tap targets, a slim persistent **Donate** dock, full-bleed vertical rescue photos. Mobile-first storytelling is the single biggest lever — the rescue-story format was practically designed for a phone.

---

### Guardrails (your keep/kill list, locked)
**Keep:** weighted scroll · large photography · editorial storytelling · negative space · type hierarchy · restraint.
**Never:** preloader · scroll-lock · hidden navigation · monochrome · experimental interactions — and **never hide the animal**; empathy needs the face (the one thing Furuya does that RKM must invert).

### Where this is concept vs. committed
This is **creative direction**, not implemented code. The restrained editorial changes already implemented (Hero, What We Do, Tobler, Why Trust) are the *first rung* of this same ladder. The rescue-story experience is a **new page/template** — a bigger build that would follow its own review + approval, not a homepage edit.
