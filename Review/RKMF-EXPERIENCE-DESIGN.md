# RKM Foundation — Experience Design
### Concept: "The Ones Who Didn't Walk Past" · ready for wireframes

> Written from three chairs at once: an Awwwards juror (does it *execute* a great idea?), a first-time donor (do I trust and act?), an animal lover (do I feel it?). Copy is RKM's real voice; `[NEEDS DATA]` marks where a real name/number/photo brings a beat to full power. The guiding principle: **in a sea of loud, pleading NGO sites, RKM's restraint is the spectacle.** What you *don't* do is the experience.

---

## 0. THE EMOTIONAL ARC (the spine everything hangs on)

Pause → Confidence → Respect → Clarity → **Awe (peak)** → Agency → Action → Belonging.

The whole site is one held breath that releases at the moment you decide you're "one of them." Nothing competes with that arc. Every section earns its place by advancing it.

---

## 1. HOMEPAGE EXPERIENCE

### Frame 0 — Arrival (full viewport, before any scroll)
**What you see.** One real rescued animal, photographed at **eye level**, in the warm low light of a night vigil (lamp-light copper over near-ink). It is *still*. It is not crying, not caged, not pleading — it is simply *present*, looking just past you. Enormous quiet. No nav clutter, no carousel, no banner.

**Copy hierarchy.**
- Line 1 (settles first, alone): **"Most people walk past."**
- Line 2 (lands a half-beat later, completing the thought): **"We're who they call when someone doesn't."** — editorial serif, large.
- One CTA, quiet, lower-thumb: **"Don't walk past →"**
- (No "Donate Now" shouting. The CTA is a moral stance, not a transaction.)

**What unfolds.** Almost nothing — and that's the point. The only motion is line 2 settling in after line 1, so the sentence *completes in your mind* a fraction after you've started reading. The animal holds your gaze. 

**Visual hierarchy.** Animal's eyes → line 1 → line 2 → CTA. Everything else is air.

**What you feel.** A pattern-break. You came expecting a charity asking for money; instead a quiet human sentence stops you. Not guilt — *recognition*. You pause. (Juror note: the calm is the craft — it reads as confidence, the opposite of the desperate NGO template.)

### Frame 1 — The founding truth
**What you see.** The animal recedes; an editorial column opens. RKM's real origin, first person, unhurried.

**Copy.** "Years ago, our dog Tobler fell ill, and we spent long nights beside him at a vet clinic. What we kept noticing wasn't the other patients — it was the people at the door. Strangers, carrying a dog or a cat they'd found hurt on the road. Not because it was theirs. Because they couldn't walk past it."
- **Giant pull-quote breaks the column:** *"They couldn't walk past it."*

**What unfolds.** The text arrives at a *reading pace* — like someone telling you something true, quietly, one thought at a time. The pull-quote glows in lamp-light copper.

**What you feel.** You're being told the origin in confidence. Trust forms — because it's specific, human, and not about a logo.

### Frame 2 — The honesty turn
**Copy (real RKM voice).** "We'd rather grow slowly and keep every promise than grow fast and break one. We're small, family-funded, and honest about it."

**What you feel.** Respect. This is the differentiator beat — an NGO that *under*-claims. In a category full of inflation, candour is magnetic.

### Frame 3 — What we do: "Four ways we show up"
**What you see.** Four eye-level photographs, revealed one at a time as you move: **Rescue · Feed · Heal · Shelter.**
**Copy (one line each).** "Rescue — wherever an animal is hurt." · "Feed — every day, for every animal in our care." · "Heal — wounds dressed, surgeries funded." · "Shelter — a safe place to rest."
**Visual hierarchy.** Photo leads, the verb anchors, the line supports.
**What you feel.** Clarity. In ten seconds you now know exactly what they do — concretely, not abstractly.

### Frame 4 — TOBLER'S LEDGER (the peak)
**What you see.** The screen becomes a quiet **wall of names.** At the top, pinned: **"Tobler · #00 · Not a rescue. The reason."** Then the names of real animals RKM has helped, each with a single-word outcome: *Recovered · Adopted · Released · Resting.*

**What unfolds.** As you scroll, names rise gently into focus. Tap or hover a name and one line of that animal's story appears — where they were found, what happened, where they are now. The lamp-light warms toward **first light (dawn)** the further down the ledger you go — you literally move from the night of rescue toward the dawn of recovery. `[NEEDS DATA: real animal names + outcomes + one-line stories]`

**Visual hierarchy.** Type *is* the image. No photos required for the wall to work; photos enrich individual cards.

**What you feel.** Awe — not at a number, but at **density of real promises kept.** This is the memory you leave with. (Juror note: this is the executed idea — it replaces the clichéd spinning counter with something only RKM could build, and it turns smallness into intimacy.)

### Frame 5 — The mirror (donor-as-hero turn)
**Copy.** "You can't be everywhere a hurt animal is. But you can be the reason someone doesn't walk past." 
**What you feel.** Agency. The site stops talking about itself and hands *you* the identity. You're invited to *become* one of the ones who don't walk past.

### Frame 6 — The ask
Calm, monthly-first, with one real testimonial beside it and an honest "where your money goes." (Detailed in §2.)
**What you feel.** Acting is easy, safe, and meaningful — the natural next step, not a hard pivot.

### Frame 7 — Trust spine + honest close
Real credentials, address, the quiet "we're small and we keep our promises" note, footer.
**What you feel.** Settled confidence. You leave either having given, or *remembering the one you couldn't walk past.*

## 2. DONATE EXPERIENCE

**Intent:** not a checkout — the moment you *join the ones who didn't walk past.*

**Headline.** "You didn't walk past. Here's what that does."

**Left column (the why).** A compact Tobler line + one real monthly-donor testimonial (a named person, their words). It keeps the emotion present while you decide.

**Right column (the act).** 
- **Frequency:** monthly is the *default*, framed not as "recurring billing" but as **"Stay with them."** One-time is one tap away, framed as "Give once."
- **Amount:** ascending tiers, each tied to a concrete outcome `[NEEDS DATA: real cost math]`; the second-lowest is pre-selected; a custom field shows its impact live.
- **Reassurance row:** "Where your money goes" `[NEEDS DATA: fund %]`, plus an optional "I'll cover the fee so my whole gift reaches them."
- **The button speaks the identity:** "Stay with them — ₹___/month" (or "Give ₹___ once").

**Emotional micro-arc within the form:** chosen, not processed → staying, not transacting → welcomed.

**Thank-you (not a dead end).** "You're one of them now." Confirms the specific impact of *this* gift; if one-time, offers the one-tap "make it monthly — stay with them"; invites a low-friction share ("tell someone who doesn't walk past either"); sets the expectation that **a name will come to you** — an animal you helped, added to the Ledger. `[NEEDS DATA]`

**What you feel across it:** welcomed into a group, not thanked by a vendor.

---

## 3. TOBLER'S LEDGER EXPERIENCE (as its own destination)

**Entry.** "Every animal we ever kept a promise to." Tobler #00 pinned as origin.

**Browsing.** Names are the texture of the page. A quiet filter by outcome (Recovered / Adopted / Released / Resting). Tap a name → a still, reverent card: photo if it exists, one-line story, date found, outcome. No metrics, no "share" spam — dignity first.

**The light.** The further you read (the more promises), the more the page warms from night toward dawn. Reading the ledger *is* the journey from rescue to release.

**The personal thread.** After you give, "your" animal appears in the Ledger — or you're told one will be added and its name sent to you. Your gift becomes a *name you know*, not a receipt number. `[NEEDS DATA]`

**What you feel:** reverence, intimacy, permanence — the opposite of a counter ticking up.

---

## 4. MOBILE EXPERIENCE (designed first, for the thumb and the street)

Most RKM donors arrive on a phone, often mid-scroll on social. Mobile isn't a shrink — it's the primary stage.

- **Hero:** the animal fills the screen; the line sits in the lower thumb-zone; one CTA. The stillness reads *even louder* on a small, busy device.
- **Story & honesty:** single-column, paced reveals; the pull-quote fills the width.
- **Four ways:** full-bleed photo cards you flick through.
- **Ledger:** a vertical register you *flick* like turning pages of a names-book; tap any name to expand. The flick gesture itself feels like leafing through a record of the kept.
- **Donate:** monthly default; UPI / GPay surfaced first (thumb-primary); amount tiers as large tap targets; the fewest possible fields; the identity button always within thumb reach.

**What you feel:** calm, fast, one-handed, never overwhelmed — care, not clutter.

---

## 5. FIRST-TIME VISITOR JOURNEY

**Arc:** curiosity → trust → belief → identity → action *or* memorable exit.
1. Lands cold (social/search), expecting another charity. The **hero pattern-break** stops the scroll.
2. The **origin story** earns trust fast — specific, human, humble.
3. **Tobler's Ledger** makes them *believe* — real names, not slogans.
4. The **mirror** hands them an identity: "someone who doesn't walk past."
5. They give — or they leave. **Even non-donors leave with the brand lodged** ("the one you couldn't walk past"), which is how memory becomes a later gift.

**Felt:** "This one is different. I'll remember it."

---

## 6. RETURNING DONOR JOURNEY (known, not yet monthly)

The site recognises them and **skips the full pitch.**
- **Greeting:** "Welcome back. The animals you helped are still here."
- It surfaces an outcome tied to their last gift, or a new name to meet `[NEEDS DATA]`.
- The ask shifts to **"Give again"** or, gently, **"Stay with them — make it monthly."**

**Felt:** remembered and appreciated, invited one step deeper without pressure.

---

## 7. MONTHLY DONOR (GUARDIAN) JOURNEY

They are treated as **inside the circle**, not a prospect.
- **Frame:** "You're one of the ones who don't walk past — every day."
- They hold a **name that's theirs** (their sponsored animal) and receive its updates; the experience is less *ask*, more *belonging + news from the field*.
- Occasional "your impact this month" and a soft, optional "add another name."

**Felt:** belonging and quiet pride — the vigil continues, and they're in it.

---

## Notes for the design team
- **The signature restraint is non-negotiable:** the hero must stay quiet. The temptation to add motion/particles will kill the concept. The experience peaks at *stillness* (hero) and *density* (Ledger), not effects.
- **`[NEEDS DATA]` to reach full power:** real named animals + outcomes + a few photos (Ledger), real cost-per-gift math (donate tiers), fund-allocation % (reassurance), the one-line mission, and consented donor names for testimonials (most already exist).
- **Wireframe order suggestion:** Homepage Frames 0→4 first (they carry the concept), then the Donate experience, then the Ledger destination, then the three journeys as state-variations.

**This is the experience layer of "The Ones Who Didn't Walk Past." Research is frozen; from here it's wireframes → build.**
