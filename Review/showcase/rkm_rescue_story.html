Rescue №118 · Thane · March 2026

The week Bruno learned to trust again.

He was found by the side of the Ghodbunder road — a fractured leg, a wary look, and no one coming back for him. This is what happened next.

A call came in just after 9pm. A young street dog, hit by a passing vehicle, dragging a back leg, refusing to let anyone near. By the time our team reached him, he had wedged himself under a parked truck — frightened, in pain, and certain that people meant harm.

It took forty minutes and a packet of biscuits before he let a hand rest on his head. We named him Bruno on the drive to the vet.

“He didn’t need much. He needed someone to not walk past.”

The X-ray confirmed a clean fracture. Surgery, plating, two weeks of cage rest, and a course of antibiotics. None of it cheap — but all of it possible, because donors had already given before Bruno ever existed to us.

₹8,240funded Bruno’s surgery and two weeks of recovery — given by 37 people who’ll never meet him.

By the end of the second week he was standing. By the third, he was leaning his whole weight against the kennel door every time a volunteer walked past — the universal dog language for stay.

Bruno is one animal. On any given week there are a dozen more — each one a phone call, a fracture, a packet of biscuits, a name given on a drive to the vet.

There’s another Bruno on the road tonight.
