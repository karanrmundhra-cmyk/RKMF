How we recreate the feeling for RKM (not the implementation)

Counter → impact counter (animals, not ego). Poster assembly → headline word-reveal over a grayscale rescue photo. Inertia scroll → same Lenis feel. Parallax → hero image drifts behind the message. Each effect must serve emotion / trust / impact / storytelling — never decoration.

Part 3 · tooling decision

Technology stack (and why)

My standalone showcase files can match the reference’s motion quality by loading production libraries from a CDN. They’re free, MIT-licensed, client-side, and used only inside these local mockups — they never touch production, Razorpay, or the live site. All effects degrade gracefully and honour prefers-reduced-motion.

ToolWhyAlternativesPerformanceMobileAccessibility
GSAP + ScrollTriggerIndustry standard for scrubbed/pinned scroll timelines; reliable cross-browserMotion One (lighter, less scrub power); Framer Motion (React-only); IntersectionObserver (our fallback)GPU-friendly transforms; ~50kb gzSolid; keep effects modestAll animations skipped on reduced-motion
LenisThe smooth inertia-scroll “feel” of the referenceLocomotive Scroll (heavier); native scroll (no inertia)Tiny, rAF-drivenFalls back to native momentumDisabled on reduced-motion (native scroll restored)
SplitTypeSplits headlines into words for staggered revealsManual span wrappingNegligibleFineText stays selectable; re-shown if motion off

Fallback proven in the build: if a library fails to load, content is fully visible (we only hide-for-animation once GSAP confirms it loaded).

Part 5

Three concepts — storyboards

Each: static design, motion storyboard, interaction storyboard, mobile storyboard, tech stack, complexity, performance. Homepages are built for all three; Concept B’s inner pages are built too; A & C inner pages are storyboarded here.

Concept A — Minimal Editorial  ▶ open home

StaticWhite page, black type, gold hairlines, huge whitespace, small framed grayscale photos. Type does the work. MotionRestrained: word-reveal headline, soft fades, gold rules that draw in, count-ups. Inertia scroll. InteractionQuiet hovers (underline, subtle image zoom), anchor scrolling. Minimal. MobileSingle column, hairlines persist, thumbnails drop, type scales down gracefully. TechGSAP + ScrollTrigger + Lenis + SplitType. ComplexityLow PerformanceLightest of the three (few full-bleed images).
Recommended · fully built

Concept B — Premium Storytelling  ▶ home · story · donate

StaticBlack-dominant, full-bleed grayscale animal photography, gold accents, oversized type, poster moments. MotionHero parallax + scroll-progress “animals reached” counter, headline word-reveal, image clip-reveals, big count-ups, monthly band. InteractionPhoto tiles scale + reveal a line on hover; monthly/one-time toggle on donate; live progress bars. MobileFull-bleed hero holds; counter hides; grids collapse to 1–2 col; tap targets enlarged. TechGSAP + ScrollTrigger + Lenis + SplitType. ComplexityMedium PerformanceImage-heavy — needs optimised grayscale assets; parallax kept to transforms.

Concept C — Impact Documentary  ▶ open home

StaticWhite/black documentary; big gold numbers; photo captions like field records; allocation bar; cost table. MotionCount-ups as the lead motif, clip-reveals on captioned images, allocation bar fill. Measured, not flashy. InteractionCaption reveals, hover on data rows, anchor jumps to proof. Restrained. MobileMetrics stack 2-up; cost table becomes single column; captions remain legible. TechGSAP + ScrollTrigger + Lenis (lighter use). ComplexityMedium PerformanceLight; shines once real verified numbers replace samples.

Part 7

Recommendation

Concept B — Premium Storytelling

It is the only direction that fully honours the brief’s first rule — the rescued animal is the hero — while recreating the reference’s motion feeling (inertia scroll, scroll-progress counter, word-reveal headlines, parallax, clip reveals). It scores highest on emotion and mission fit, and it can absorb Concept C’s proof devices (counters, allocation, documentary captions) as RKM’s real numbers come online — so it’s the strongest long-term home, not just the prettiest.

Built for review: B home, B rescue story, B donate (+ A and C homepages, and A/C inner pages storyboarded above). No production code, nothing deployed — awaiting your approval of a concept before anything is implemented.
