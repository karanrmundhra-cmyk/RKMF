Stage 3

Three showcase concepts

Concept A — Minimal Editorial

Monochrome-leaning, type-forward, austere. The most literal translation of the reference.
HomepageSand/ink palette, B&W or duotone photography, oversized type, huge whitespace, one CTA.
Rescue storyLong-form, typographic, sparse imagery, quiet pull-quotes.
DonateStripped, single-column, restrained — type does the persuading.
Recommended · built

Concept B — Premium Storytelling

Full-bleed warm animal photography + editorial type + a scroll-driven emotional arc. Craft of the reference, warmth of the cause.
HomepageCinematic animal hero, impact counters, four-ways grid, a live case (Bruno), Guardian Circle, trust + testimonial.

Stage 5

Final recommendation

Concept B — Premium Storytelling

It is the only direction that fully honours the brief’s first rule: the rescued animal is the hero. It borrows Victor Furuya’s editorial craft — oversized type, full-bleed photography, scroll-driven motion, deep negative space, emotive one-liners — but points all of it at the animal and the act of giving, so it never feels like a designer portfolio.

It also serves the actual goal best: emotion (the recovery arc) + proof (impact counters, live cases) + a clear path to monthly giving — the engine the live site under-uses. Concept A risks feeling cold for this cause; Concept C is excellent but depends on real data and can read as a report. Concept B can absorb C’s proof elements (counters, before/after, allocation) as RKM’s real numbers come online — so it’s the safest long-term home, not just the prettiest.

Recommendation: proceed with Concept B. The three built mockups show the direction. No code beyond these standalone showcases — awaiting your approval before anything is implemented.
