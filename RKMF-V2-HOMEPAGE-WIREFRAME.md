## Section-by-section (hero → footer)

**0. Slim nav** — warm-white, serif wordmark, 4 links + gold pill **Donate**. Solidifies on scroll. Sticky gold donate bar on mobile.

**1. Hero — light editorial split**
- Left: gold eyebrow ("Animal Welfare · India · Since 2014"), serif headline **"They can't ask. So we answer."**, one warm sentence, gold **Save a life today** + ghost **Meet the rescued**.
- Right: one rounded rescue portrait (light, soft shadow, gentle zoom-in) with a floating stat chip ("1,260+ animals rescued").
- *Not* a dark full-bleed. Light, airy, immediately warm.

**2. Trust strip** — ivory band: **12A · 80G · CSR-1 · DARPAN**. Quiet credibility, on-domain.

**3. Our Story (Tobler)** — image + serif prose + gold pull-quote. The origin, warmly told. One CTA: read the story.

**4. Meet the Animals** — four named portrait cards (Tobler, Sheru, Luna, Gauri): status chip, rescue date, one-line story, recovery bar, "Sponsor →". *This is the heart — the thing Framer and production both miss.*

**5. Before / After** — interactive drag slider, "Drag to see what your kindness does." Proof, made playable.

**6. Impact counters** — the **one cocoa (warm-dark) band**: gold count-up numbers (meals, rescues, surgeries, sheltered). Scale you feel; drama without going dark everywhere.

**7. Sponsor an Animal** — three monthly tiers (Feed ₹1,000 / **Heal ₹2,500, featured** / Shelter ₹5,000). Monthly framed as a relationship with one animal.

**8. "Your ₹1,000 did this"** — three concrete impact blocks tying rupees to outcomes + primary donate CTA + security/80G trust line.

**9. Give your way (faith-based)** — warm light cards: Dāna & Seva, Zakat & Sadaqah, Ahimsa in action, Simple kindness. Borrowed from Framer's best idea — culturally resonant, emotional, distinctly Indian. (Condensed, tasteful, not 10 faiths.)

**10. Testimonials** — three real, named supporters with faces/cities (Raghav Daga · Shweta Desai · Aditya Mohatta) on a soft-stone band. Human trust.

**11. CSR** — light split: Schedule VII messaging + audit badges + programme image + gold CTA. Corporate-grade without going cold.

**12. Closing donate band** — warm gold-tint gradient, emotional one-liner, donate + sponsor.

**13. Footer** — cocoa, "**Subscribe to Hope**" email capture, explore/contact columns, and the full on-domain registration line (E-30560 · PAN · 12A · 80G · CSR · DARPAN).

## Emotional flow — the first 30 seconds (SEE · FEEL · THINK · DO)

| Time | Section | SEE | FEEL | THINK | DO |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0–4s | Hero | A warm portrait of one rescued animal + "They can't ask. So we answer." | Calm, moved, "this is beautiful and kind" | "This is serious and premium, and it's about an animal" | Notice the gold **Donate** |
| 4–10s | Trust strip + Story | 12A/80G/CSR badges; Tobler's photo & quote | Reassured + warmed | "Real, registered, and it began with a real family and a real dog" | Keep scrolling |
| 10–18s | Meet the Animals | Four *named* animals with recovery bars | Connection — "these are individuals" | "I could help *that* one — Sheru, 72% recovered" | Hover a card; consider sponsoring |
| 18–24s | Before/After + Counters | A drag-to-reveal recovery; 48,200 meals, 1,260 rescues | Proof + scale; trust hardens | "It actually works, and at scale" | Drag the slider |
| 24–30s | Your ₹1,000 did this | "₹10,000 = a life-saving surgery" | Clarity + agency | "I know exactly what my money does" | Tap **Give now** / **Sponsor Sheru** |

By 30 seconds they've moved from *seeing* an animal → *feeling* connection → *trusting* the proof → *acting*.

## The most important answer

> **If someone donates ₹10,000 after 60 seconds on the website, exactly what convinced them?**

Not one thing — a sequence that earned a large gift fast:

1. **The first image made them care** — a single warm rescue portrait, not a logo or a stock smile. Emotion before anything else.
2. **The credibility was instant and quiet** — 12A · 80G · CSR · DARPAN, on-domain, no shouting. The "is this legit / is it tax-deductible?" question was answered before it was asked.
3. **An animal became a someone** — a *named* individual (Sheru, rescued April 2026, 72% recovered) turned an abstract cause into one life they could personally save.
4. **The proof was undeniable** — the before/after they dragged themselves, and counters showing 1,260 rescues and 430 surgeries: this works, and at scale.
5. **The ₹10,000 was made concrete** — "₹10,000 = a life-saving surgery." They weren't donating to an org; they were buying a specific outcome — *the* operation between goodbye and a second chance.
6. **Others already trusted it** — real named donors with faces said it felt personal and transparent.
7. **It felt premium, so the money felt safe** — warm-light editorial craft signalled competence and care; a ₹10,000 gift felt at home here, not risky.

**In one line:** they gave ₹10,000 because in 60 seconds the site made them *feel* one animal's story, *proved* it could heal that animal, *showed* exactly what ₹10,000 buys, and *looked* trustworthy enough to hand real money to. Emotion opened the wallet; proof and polish justified the size.
